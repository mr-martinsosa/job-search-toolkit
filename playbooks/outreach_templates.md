# Outreach Templates — the referral power move

Referrals convert ~10× a cold apply, so this is the highest-value asset in the toolkit after
the resume itself. Four contact archetypes, a constant 3-sentence structure, **300-character
hard cap** (LinkedIn connection-request limit). The contact type changes the *emphasis*, not
the structure. Fill the blanks and go.

---

## Step 1 — Identify targets (do this manually from the company's team page / LinkedIn)
- The **hiring manager** of the team
- The assigned **recruiter**
- 2–3 **peers** in similar roles ← the indirect referral path
- The **interviewer** (only once an interview is scheduled)

## Step 2 — Pick the primary target
The person who'd benefit most from you being on the team. For a referral, that's usually a **peer**.

---

## The four archetypes (S1 / S2 / S3)

### Recruiter — *talent/sourcing*
- **S1 (Fit):** direct match criteria — role, relevant experience, availability, location.
- **S2 (Proof):** data that answers their screen before they ask.
- **S3 (CTA, verbatim):** *"Happy to share my CV if this aligns with what you're looking for."*

### Hiring Manager — *leads the team*
- **S1 (Hook):** a specific challenge their team faces (from the JD, blog, or news).
- **S2 (Proof):** your biggest quantifiable achievement showing you've solved that shape of problem.
- **S3 (CTA, verbatim):** *"Would love to hear how your team is approaching [specific challenge]."*

### Peer — *similar role on the team* ← the high-value one
- **S1 (Interest):** a genuine reference to their work — a talk, post, repo, or publication.
- **S2 (Connection):** something *you're* doing in the same space (NOT a job pitch).
- **S3 (CTA, verbatim):** *"I've been working on similar problems — would love to hear your take on [topic]."*
- **⚠ DO NOT ask for a job.** The referral happens naturally if the conversation flows.

### Interviewer — *pre-interview, date known*
- **S1 (Research):** reference something specific from their work or trajectory.
- **S2 (Context):** a light connection to your experience in that area.
- **S3 (CTA, verbatim):** *"Looking forward to our conversation on [date]."*
- Light tone, not desperate. The goal is to show you prepared.

---

## Message rules (apply to all archetypes)
- **Max 300 characters** (LinkedIn connection-request limit).
- No corporate-speak. No *"I'm passionate about…"*.
- Say something that makes them *want* to respond.
- **Never** share your phone number.
- The contact type changes the **emphasis**, not the structure.

---

## Build your own proof bank (so each message is fast to instantiate)

Write 3–4 one-line proof points you can defend, each tuned to a role family. Pick the line that
matches the archetype/role. Examples (replace with your real, quantified wins):

- Build-with-AI: *"I shipped an LLM-powered in-app assistant (auth-gated, rate-limited) in a React/TS app I built."*
- Performance: *"I cut median page load from 3.1s to 0.9s for ~40k weekly users."*
- Reliability: *"I ran a zero-downtime Postgres migration of 2M rows on a live billing service."*
- Identity: *"mid-level full-stack + AI engineer, full-stack React/Node + LLM integration, available now."*

**Recruiter (≤300 ch):**
> Hi [name] — saw the [Role] opening. I'm a full-stack + AI engineer (React/Node + LLM integration), [N] yrs, available now. Happy to share my CV if this aligns with what you're looking for.

**Peer (≤300 ch) — no ask:**
> Hi [name] — really liked your [post/talk/repo] on [topic]. I've been building [your current full-stack+AI side project] in the same space. Would love to hear your take on [specific thing].

**Hiring Manager (≤300 ch):**
> Hi [name] — [Role] caught my eye; looks like [team] is tackling [challenge from JD]. I [your one-line proof of solving that shape of problem]. Would love to hear how your team is approaching [challenge].

---

## Tie it into the tracker
For each lead you decide to pursue, note the archetype/target in `leads.csv`'s notes cell —
e.g. `referral: peer (Dana, ask re: agents)`. The word `referral`/`intro` lights up the
dashboard's referral marker, so your warm leads surface at a glance.
